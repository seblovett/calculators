About Crosstalk

Backward (near-end) crosstalk KB appears at the source end of the victim trace and grows with coupled length until the line delay exceeds half the rise time, after which it saturates. Forward (far-end) crosstalk KF appears at the far end of the victim and increases proportionally with coupled length.

In a stripline (symmetric), forward crosstalk is near-zero because the inductive and capacitive components cancel. In microstrip they do not cancel, leading to non-zero forward crosstalk. The 3W rule (centre-to-centre spacing ≥ 3× trace width) reduces EMI radiation and coupling to below −10 dB.
